FAQ

What's PolyView?

PolyView indexes Cal Poly's 2013-2015 Course Catalog. Current instructors (and their Polyratings) are also available for searching, and we've made it easy to find what you need on the campus map.

We aggregate this info and connect it in cool new ways. Use, say, CPE 315 as an example. Click around and see where you end up.

Want to see your favorite professors' course schedules next quarter? Rushing to a room on the first day of class? Need to know what courses you can take after another course? We've got you covered.

What can I search for? How should I search?

Quickstart guide!

  • Enter a subject to see its courses.
    • Try "English" or the course prefix "ENGL".
  • Enter a building to get relevant maps.
    • "Building 47" will get you around the faculty offices Maze.
    • A search like "math building" might be of help too.
  • Enter a course number or title to search for it.
    • Try "AG 243" or "rodeo".
    • Remember a number but can't come up with the subject? You can do something like "101".

How up-to-date is PolyView?

We retrieve new course schedules and Polyratings at the beginning of each quarter.

This should be fine for many cases, but please refer to PASS to finalize your schedule.

Where's this data from?

A few websites:

  • schedules.calpoly.edu
    • Course schedule
    • Instructor contact details
  • catalog.calpoly.edu
    • Course descriptions
  • maps.calpoly.edu
    • Campus map
  • afd.calpoly.edu
    • Building floorplans
  • polyratings.com
    • Polyratings reviews

Note that PolyView doesn't generate any content. Concerns about course or instructor data or the campus map should be directed to Cal Poly. Concerns about instructor ratings should be directed to Polyratings.

If their data looks right but we didn't display something correctly, you can report a problem for instructor Polyratings (see the next question) or send us an email if something else is bugging you (see the last question).

An instructor's Polyratings are missing or for a different person.

Sorry, names are tricky.

The likely culprit is a typo on Polyratings or use of an obscure nickname. It's also possible that an instructor has not yet been rated. If something looks off and we should have different ratings, click the 'Report a Problem' button. We'll use that info to improve our matching algorithms.

It's also worth noting that this shouldn't be an issue between instructors and courses. We use their email for that.
